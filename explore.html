"Tell Basta.. Capital of Joy & City of the Gods"

Sharqia. Cult center of the cat goddess "Bastet". A once-vibrant city known for its loud festivals, its ruins still hide secrets from the 22nd Dynasty.

"Lahun Pyramid.. The Mystery of Fayum & The Thieves' Maze"

Fayum - Middle Kingdom. A mud-brick masterpiece built by "Senusret II", featuring a complex interior design and an underground maze brilliantly engineered to mislead tomb robbers.

"Gebel el-Silsila.. The Great Quarries of the Pharaohs"

North of Aswan. Not just an archaeological site, but the largest sandstone quarry. Here, stones for the greatest temples were cut, and its walls are adorned with rare rock-cut shrines.

"Serabit el-Khadim.. Temple of the Lady of Turquoise"

South Sinai. An isolated temple atop a towering mountain peak, where the Pharaohs secretly extracted turquoise under the protection of the goddess Hathor.

"Tuna el-Gebel.. Catacombs of Mystery & The Martyr of Love"

Minya. A necropolis brilliantly blending ancient Egyptian and Greek art, housing massive underground catacombs and the famous mummy of "Isadora".

"Al-Qasr Village.. The Ancient Mud Maze"

Dakhla Oasis. A complete Islamic city that survived time, featuring roofed streets entirely built of mud-brick and doum palm wood.

"Sakakini Palace.. Legends of Italian Rococo"

Cairo (1897 AD). An architectural masterpiece standing as a myth-haunted palace, featuring hundreds of statues and artistic details unmatched in modern architecture.
